Bumps on the surface of the tongue that provide friction

(A) ** Papillae

(B) Villi

(C) Rugae

(D) Fundus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The small bumps on your tongue are called papillae.

Concept note-2: -Fungiform papillae are the small bumps located on the top and sides of your tongue. Theyâ€™re the same color as the rest of your tongue and, under normal circumstances, are unnoticeable. They give your tongue a rough texture, which helps you eat. They also contain taste buds and temperature sensors.

Concept note-3: -The dorsal surface of the mammalian tongue is covered with four kinds of papillae, fungiform, circumvallate, foliate and filiform papillae.

Concept note-4: -It is thought that foods with high acidity or high sugar content may cause inflammation of the fungiform papillae, leading to the raised bumps on the tongue. Spicy foods and food allergies are also thought to play a role.
