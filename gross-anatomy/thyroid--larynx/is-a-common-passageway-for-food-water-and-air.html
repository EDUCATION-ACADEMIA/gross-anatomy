Is a common passageway for food, water and air

(A) ** Pharynx

(B) Esophagus

(C) Larynopharynx

(D) Throat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The common passage for food and air is pharynx. Pharynx is the musculomembranous cavity behind the nose and mouth, connecting them to the oesophagus and larynx.

Concept note-2: -Complete answer: The common passage for food and air is the oropharynx.

Concept note-3: -Air, food and liquid all pass through this common passage, the oropharynx. The two passages separate again here, in the hypopharynx. Food and liquid pass backward into the esophagus on their way to the stomach. Air passes forward through the larynx and into the trachea, on its way to the lungs.

Concept note-4: -(FAYR-inx) The hollow tube inside the neck that starts behind the nose and ends at the top of the trachea (windpipe) and esophagus (the tube that goes to the stomach). The pharynx is about 5 inches long, depending on body size. Also called throat.

Concept note-5: -Reason: Pharynx acts as the common passage for food and air.
