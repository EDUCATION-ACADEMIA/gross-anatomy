Which is NOT a way to exercise your heart?

(A) Running

(B) ** Sitting on the couch

(C) Swimming

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Avoid too much isometric exercises such as pushups and situps. Isometric exercises involve straining muscles against other muscles or an immovable object. Don’t exercise outdoors when it is too cold, hot or humid without checking with your doctor first.

Concept note-2: -Aerobic Exercise How much: Ideally, at least 30 minutes a day, at least five days a week. Examples: Brisk walking, running, swimming, cycling, playing tennis and jumping rope. Heart-pumping aerobic exercise is the kind that doctors have in mind when they recommend at least 150 minutes per week of moderate activity.

Concept note-3: -Sit upright with your feet flat on the floor, cross your arms and reach for your shoulders. B. Without moving your hips, turn your upper body to the left as far as is comfortable. Hold for 5 seconds.

Concept note-4: -Stretch neck and shoulders. Sit-stand (core, legs, glutes). Triceps dips (arms, shoulders, chest, core). Chest squeeze (pectorals). Leg scissors (core). Core twist with pillow (core, back, flexibility). Squat and reach (glutes, legs, core, upper body). Clam shell (thighs, hips). More items •02-Apr-2020
