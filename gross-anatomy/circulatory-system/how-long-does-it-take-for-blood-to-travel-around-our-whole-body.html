How long does it take for blood to travel around our whole body?

(A) 5 minutes

(B) ** 20 seconds

(C) 1 minute

(D) 40 seconds

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It takes around 20 seconds for a red blood cell to travel around the body. The circulatory system is an essential part of our body.

Concept note-2: -Amazingly, it only takes about 20 seconds for one red blood cell to go round the whole body. Red blood cells last about 4 months before your body makes new ones. We’ve talked about the blood in your system collecting oxygen and delivering it all around the body, but it also does another important job.

Concept note-3: -How long does it take blood to circulate through the body? On average, it takes about 45 seconds for blood to circulate from the heart, all around the body, and back to the heart again. An average adult’s heart beats more than 100, 000 times a day.

Concept note-4: -Your body has about 5.6 liters (6 quarts) of blood. This 5.6 liters of blood circulates through the body three times every minute. In one day, the blood travels a total of 19, 000 km (12, 000 miles)-that’s four times the distance across the US from coast to coast.

Concept note-5: -The average circulation time in normal resting adults for the blood to travel from the arm vein, through the heart and lungs, and up to the mouth, is 13 seconds, as determined by sodium dehydrocholate. The normal range is 10 to 16 seconds.
