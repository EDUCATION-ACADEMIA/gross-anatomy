GROSS ANATOMY

BODY CAVITIES

[SOURCES]
The terms relating to body planes, directions, and cavities are used frequently in the study of humans

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The dorsal cavity is one long, continuous cavity. The abdominal cavity is frequently divided into six sections. The kidneys and adrenal glands are in the retroperitoneal space behind the abdominal cavity. The terms relating to body planes, directions, and cavities are used frequently in the study of human anatomy.

Concept note-2: -The midsagittal plane divides the body into right and left. The dorsal plane divides the body into top and bottom, and the lateral plane divides the body into front and back. The body cavities are hollow locations within the body that house body organs. For example, the pleural cavity contains the lungs.

Concept note-3: -There are three basic reference planes used in anatomy: the sagittal plane, the coronal plane, and the transverse plane.

Concept note-4: -Understanding anatomical directional terms and body planes will make it easier to study anatomy. It will help you to be able to visualize positional and spatial locations of structures and navigate directionally from one area to another.

Concept note-5: -There are three planes of the body: Coronal (frontal) plane: separates the front (anterior) and back (posterior) of the body. Sagittal (longitudinal) plane: separates the left and right sides of the body. Transverse (axial) plane: separates the upper (superior) and lower (inferior) halves of the body.
