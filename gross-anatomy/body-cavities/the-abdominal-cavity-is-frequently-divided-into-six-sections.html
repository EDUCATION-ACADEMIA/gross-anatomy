GROSS ANATOMY

BODY CAVITIES

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

The abdominal cavity is frequently divided into six sections

(A) true

(B) ** false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The abdominal cavity is frequently divided into six sections. The kidneys and adrenal glands are in the retroperitoneal space behind the abdominal cavity. The terms relating to body planes, directions, and cavities are used frequently in the study of human anatomy. Bones surround vital organs to protect them.

Concept note-2: -On the sides of the abdomen the other six regions are the left and right hypochondriac regions, on either side of the epigastrium; the left and right lumbar flank regions, on either side of the umbilical region, and the left and right iliac or inguinal regions on either side of the hypogastrium.

Concept note-3: -Anatomical terminology for body cavities: Humans have multiple body cavities, including the cranial cavity, the vertebral cavity, the thoracic cavity (containing the pericardial cavity and the pleural cavity), the abdominal cavity, and the pelvic cavity.

Concept note-4: -There are three planes of the body: Coronal (frontal) plane: separates the front (anterior) and back (posterior) of the body. Sagittal (longitudinal) plane: separates the left and right sides of the body. Transverse (axial) plane: separates the upper (superior) and lower (inferior) halves of the body.

Concept note-5: -Coronal Plane or Frontal Plane. Sagittal Plane or Lateral Plane. Axial Plane or Transverse Plane.
