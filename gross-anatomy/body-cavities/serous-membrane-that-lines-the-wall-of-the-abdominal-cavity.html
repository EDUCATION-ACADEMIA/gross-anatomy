Serous membrane that lines the wall of the abdominal cavity.

(A) parietal pleura

(B) visceral pleura

(C) ** parietal peritoneum

(D) visceral peritoneum

(E) parietal pericardium

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The peritoneum is the serous membrane that lines the abdominal cavity. It is composed of mesothelial cells that are supported by a thin layer of fibrous tissue and is embryologically derived from the mesoderm.

Concept note-2: -Your parietal peritoneum lines your abdominal and pelvic walls and your visceral peritoneum wraps around your organs.

Concept note-3: -The part of the serous membrane that lines the peritoneal cavity wall is called visceral peritoneum. A major function of serous membranes is to decrease friction. Lungs carry out an excretory function. Embryology concerns the structural changes that occur in an individual from conception through old age.

Concept note-4: -The peritoneum is a single layer of squamous mesothelial cells resting on a loose connective tissue containing blood vessels, lymphatics, and nerves. Anatomically, the peritoneum is divided into a parietal and visceral peritoneum. The parietal peritoneum lines the diaphragm, abdominal walls, and pelvic cavity.

Concept note-5: -Pleura – Surrounding the lungs in the pleural cavity. Pericardium – surrounding the heart and blood vessels in the pericardial cavity. Peritoneum – surrounding the abdominal cavity.
