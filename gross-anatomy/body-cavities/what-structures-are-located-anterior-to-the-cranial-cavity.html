What structures are located anterior to the cranial cavity?

(A) Ears

(B) ** Eyes

(C) Lungs

(D) Neck muscles

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Basal Ganglia. Cerebrum. Cerebellum. Meninges. Pineal Gland. Pituitary Gland. Spinal Cord (Grey Matter)

Concept note-2: -The anterior cranial fossa is a depression in the cranial base, which primarily supports the large frontal lobes of the cerebral hemispheres. However, in the center of the anterior cranial fossa are small depressions, the olfactory fossae, which support the olfactory bulbs (Figs. 2.1, 2.6, and 2.7).

Concept note-3: -The anterior cranial fossa is formed by the orbital part of the frontal bone, the cribriform plate and crista galli of the ethmoid bone, and the lesser wings and anterior part of the body (jugum sphenoidale and prechiasmatic sulcus) of the sphenoid bone (Standing, 2015).

Concept note-4: -The cranial cavity is the anterior portion of the dorsal cavity consisting of the space inside the skull. This cavity contains the brain, the meninges of the brain, and cerebrospinal fluid.

Concept note-5: -The frontal bone, as seen below in pink, is the anterior roof of the skull, the bone of the forehead, and extends down to be the superior portion of the orbits, the top of the eye sockets. It articulates (is connected to) with the parietal, sphenoid, ethmoid, nasal, lacrimal, maxillary, and zygomatic bones.
