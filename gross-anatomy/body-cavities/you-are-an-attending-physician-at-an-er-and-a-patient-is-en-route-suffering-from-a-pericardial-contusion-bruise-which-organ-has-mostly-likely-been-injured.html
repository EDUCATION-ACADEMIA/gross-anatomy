You are an attending physician at an ER and a patient is en route suffering from a pericardial contusion (bruise). Which organ has mostly likely been injured?

(A) lung

(B) brain

(C) ** heart

(D) kidney

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A myocardial contusion, or cardiac contusion, is a bruise on your heart muscle. Common causes include car crashes and falls. Treatment depends on the complications that may happen after this blunt cardiac injury. Recovery depends on the severity of your injury, but most people make a full recovery.

Concept note-2: -The aortic valve is most commonly involved, followed by the mitral. Such injuries usually manifest as a combination of left ventricular dysfunction and cardiogenic shock.

Concept note-3: -Blunt injury to the heart is a blow to the chest that bruises the heart muscle, tears (ruptures) the heartâ€™s walls, or damages a heart valve. People may feel that their heart is pounding or racing, feel short of breath, or have dangerously low blood pressure.

Concept note-4: -Thoracic cage. The most common injury in blunt thoracic trauma is chest wall injury, which also includes rib fractures. When bone fractures are mentioned, not only rib fractures but also clavicle and sternum fractures should be considered.
