When combining two roots and the second root begins with a vowel, use the ____ ____ of the first word part to combine the two.

(A) word root

(B) ** combining form

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When you take a word root and add a vowel it becomes a combining form. This vowel is usually an ―o‖, and it is called a combining vowel.-cyst/o-therm/o The combining vowel is used before suffixes that begin with a consonant and before another word root.

Concept note-2: -A suffix is added at the end of the word. A combining vowel will be used if the suffix begins with a consonant. A combining vowel is generally not used if the suffix begins with a vowel (there are some exceptions to this rule). If the suffix begins with a vowel, the root will attach directly to it.

Concept note-3: -When two word roots are joined, a combining vowel is always added to the first word root. For example, the term gastroenteritis combines two word roots with a suffix: when gastr (stomach) is joined with the word root enter (small intestine), a vowel is used to make the combining form gastr/o .

Concept note-4: -When two word roots are joined, the combining vowel is always used. This rule applies even when the second word root begins with a vowel. When the suffix begins with a vowel, a combining vowel is not added with the adjacent word root.
