Heel Area

(A) Plantar

(B) Popliteal

(C) Patellar

(D) ** Calcaneal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The calcaneus (heel bone) is the largest of the tarsal bones in the foot. It lies at the back of the foot (hindfoot) below the three bones that make up the ankle joint. These three bones are the: Tibia (shinbone)

Concept note-2: -Often called heel, the calcaneus is a large and strong bone that forms the back of the foot and transfers most of the body weight from the lower extremity to the ground. It is the largest of the seven articulating bones that make up the tarsus.

Concept note-3: -In humans and many other primates, the calcaneus (/kælˈkeɪniəs/; from the Latin calcaneus or calcaneum, meaning heel) or heel bone is a bone of the tarsus of the foot which constitutes the heel.

Concept note-4: -The heel is the portion of the human body that lies at the bottom-rear part of each foot. Its exterior shape is formed by the calcaneus, also known as the heel bone. The heel bone is the largest bone in the foot.

Concept note-5: -Treating heel pain regular stretching – stretching your calf muscles and plantar fascia. pain relief – using an icepack on the affected heel and taking painkillers, such as non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) wearing well fitted shoes that support and cushion your feet – running shoes are particularly useful.
