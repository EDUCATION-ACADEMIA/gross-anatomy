(A) palace

(B) omphal(o)

(C) them(about)

(D) ** eyelid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An eyelid is a fold of skin that closes over the eye to protect it. There are upper and lower eyelids. Many things can affect the eyelids or are related to the eyelid.

Concept note-2: -An eyelid is a thin fold of skin that covers and protects an eye. The levator palpebrae superioris muscle retracts the eyelid, exposing the cornea to the outside, giving vision. This can be either voluntarily or involuntarily.

Concept note-3: -The Eyelids At the top of the upper eyelid is a fold in the skin called a superior palpebral sulcus (skin crease).

Concept note-4: -Hooded eyelids have that extra skin on the upper eyelids, while double eyelids do not have skin and fat around the eyes. Because of this, the double eyelidsâ€™ crease is much more visible than that of hooded eyelids.
