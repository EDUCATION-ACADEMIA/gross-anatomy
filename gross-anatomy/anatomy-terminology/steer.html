steer

(A) female

(B) male, intact

(C) ** male, castrated

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Castration is the removal of the testicles from male animals. A bull that has been castrated is called a steer. There are various castration methods, and it is recommended that castrations be performed in calves at the youngest age possible (1), preferably within three months of age (2).

Concept note-2: -steer, also called bullock, young neutered male cattle primarily raised for beef. In the terminology used to describe the sex and age of cattle, the male is first a bull calf and if left intact becomes a bull; if castrated he becomes a steer and about two or three years grows to an ox.

Concept note-3: -The female counterpart to a bull is a cow, while a male of the species that has been castrated is a steer, ox, or bullock, although in North America, this last term refers to a young bull.

Concept note-4: -A steer is a castrated male bovine. Male bovines are castrated when they are young and before they develop the bullâ€™s physical characteristics, according to USDA. Steers are less aggressive than bulls.

Concept note-5: -Castration Defined Castration of a bull (male) calf is the process of removal or destruction of the testicles. A steer is a castrated male calf raised for beef.
