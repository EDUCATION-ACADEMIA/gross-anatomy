The shoulder is ____ to the elbow.

(A) ** proximal

(B) dorsal

(C) distal

(D) ventral

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Proximal means ‘closer to the origin of a structure’, like a limb, while distal means ‘farther away from the structure’s origin’. Thus, when comparing the shoulder to the elbow, the shoulder is proximal and the elbow is distal.

Concept note-2: -Proximal-Nearest; closer to any point of reference. The elbow is proximal to the wrist on the upper extremity.

Concept note-3: -The anatomical direction of the shoulder to the elbow is proximal. Proximal signifies proximity. If any structure is proximal to another structure, it means that it is closer to the trunk. It is different from the distal as the distal position represents away from the attachment point.

Concept note-4: -The opposite of proximal is distal, which means further away from the torso. We can reverse our shoulder and elbow phrase by saying that the elbow is distal to the shoulder.

Concept note-5: -The shoulder is considered superior (above) and proximal to the elbow, indicating that it is closer to the point of attachment.
