How many pairs of false ribs are there?

(A) ** 5

(B) 3

(C) 2

(D) 7

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -False ribs are the last five pairs of ribs that are not immediately linked to the sternum.

Concept note-2: -The lower five pairs of ribs (ribs eight to twelve) are known as false ribs because their costal cartilages do not articulate directly with the sternum. The costal cartilages of ribs eight to ten connect with the costal cartilages of the ribs above.

Concept note-3: -There are three pairs of false ribs. They are intermediate between the true ribs and the floating ribs. They are sometimes called vertebrochondral ribs. They differ from the true ribs because they do not directly articulate with the sternum.

Concept note-4: -The first seven rib pairs known as the fixed or vertebrosternal ribs are the true ribs (Latin: costae verae) as they connect directly to the sternum; the next five pairs (eighth to twelfth) are the false ribs (Latin: costae spuriae). The false ribs include both vertebrochondral ribs and vertebral ribs.
