VERTEBRAL COLUMN

[SOURCES]
C1, (1st cervical vertebra) articulates with the head

(A) Axis

(B) ** Atlas

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -atlas: the first cervical vertebra (C1), lying directly under the skull, through which the head articulates with the neck. The main connection to the vertebra below is a pivot around the odontoid process that is an upward projection of the body of the second cervical vertebra .

Concept note-2: -The atlas (plural: atlases) is the first cervical vertebra, commonly called C1. It is an atypical cervical vertebra with unique features. It articulates with the dens of the axis and the occiput, respectively allowing rotation of the head, and flexion, extension and lateral flexion of the head.

Concept note-3: -The C1 atlas is the uppermost cervical vertebra of the spine. It supports the head and-along with the C2 axis, the cervical vertebra just below it-allows the head to nod and rotate from side to side. At birth, children have softer bones.

Concept note-4: -Articulations. Superiorly the atlas articulates with the occiput to form the Atlanto-occipital joint which allows the head to nod up and down on the vertebral column.
