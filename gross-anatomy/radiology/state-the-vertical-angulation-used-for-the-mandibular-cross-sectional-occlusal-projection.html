State the vertical angulation used for the mandibular cross-sectional occlusal projection.

(A) +90 degrees

(B) -90 degrees

(C) ** 90 degrees

(D) 95 degrees

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -State the vertical angulation used for the mandibular topographic occlusal projection:-55; centered over chin. State the vertical angulation used for the mandibular cross-sectional occlusal projection: +90; centered 1 inch below chin.

Concept note-2: -The central ray (PID) is aimed with a vertical angle of-90 degrees, a horizontal angle of 0 degrees and centered on the midline of the patient near the middle of the film/phosphor plate. The film/phosphor plate can be centered in the oral cavity or positioned to a side depending on what you want to view.

Concept note-3: -The maxillary standard occlusal radiograph is made with a size 4 film/phosphor plate. The film/phosphor plate is positioned with its long axis laterally. The central ray (PID) is aimed with a vertical angle of +65 to +70 degrees, a horizontal angle of 0 degrees and centered on the bridge of the nose.

Concept note-4: -What position is the patients head when taking a mandibular cross-sectional occlusal radiograph? Patient reclined in chair with head tipped back.
