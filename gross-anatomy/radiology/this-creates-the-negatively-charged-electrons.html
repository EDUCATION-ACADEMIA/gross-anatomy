EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cathode rays come from the cathode because the cathode is charged negatively. So those rays strike and ionize the gas sample inside the container. The electrons that were ejected from gas ionization travel to the anode. These rays are electrons that are actually produced from the gas ionization inside the tube.

Concept note-2: -When we talk about cathode in chemistry, it is said to be the electrode where reduction occurs. This is common in an electrochemical cell. Here, the cathode is negative as the electrical energy that is supplied to the cell results in the decomposition of chemical compounds.

Concept note-3: -In 1897, British physicist J. J. Thomson showed that cathode rays were composed of a previously unknown negatively charged particle, which was later named the electron.

Concept note-4: -Cathode rays travel in straight lines and cast sharp shadows. Unlike light, however, ca-thode rays are attracted toward a positively charged plate. This led to the conclusion that cathode rays are negatively charged.

Concept note-5: -When cathode rays are passed through an electric field, they are deflected towards the positive plate of the electric field. Hence it can be concluded that the cathode rays are negatively charged particles.
