Who made stitches

(A) Rhazes

(B) Cat guts

(C) Me

(D) ** Hippocritis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The first known sutures are used in Egyptian times Egyptian records reveal the first historical reference to sutures being used to treat a shoulder: “Thou shouldst draw together for him his gash with stitching.”

Concept note-2: -Ancient times. The synthetic suture materials in use today are the result of surgical experience from approximately 3000 years BCE (Before Common Era). Ancient Egyptians sutured using plant fibres, hair, tendons and wool threads, which have all been found in mummified remains.

Concept note-3: -The earliest reports of surgical suture date to 3000 BC in ancient Egypt, and the oldest known suture is in a mummy from 1100 BC. A detailed description of a wound suture and the suture materials used in it is by the Indian sage and physician Sushruta, written in 500 BC.

Concept note-4: -Although he is known as Albucasis, Abu’l-Qasim or Al-Zahrawi in the West, his real name was Abu al-Qasim Khalaf Ibn Al-Abbas Al-Zahrawi.
