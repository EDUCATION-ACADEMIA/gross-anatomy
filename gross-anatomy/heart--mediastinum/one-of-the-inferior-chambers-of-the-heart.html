GROSS ANATOMY

HEART MEDIASTINUM

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

One of the inferior chambers of the heart:

(A) ** ventricle

(B) myocardium

(C) atrium

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your lower chambers are called your right ventricle and left ventricle.

Concept note-2: -The inferior surface of the right ventricle accounts for a small part of the diaphragmatic surface of the heart. It sits over the central tendon of the diaphragm and a small muscular portion of the left hemidiaphragm. The interventricular septum functions as the posterior and left wall of the right ventricle.

Concept note-3: -A typical heart has two upper and two lower chambers. The upper chambers-the right and left atria-receive incoming blood. The lower chambers-the right and left ventricles-pump blood out of your heart.

Concept note-4: -There are four chambers: the left atrium and right atrium (upper chambers), and the left ventricle and right ventricle (lower chambers). The right side of your heart collects blood on its return from the rest of your body. The blood entering the right side of your heart is low in oxygen.

Concept note-5: -The inferior tip of the heart, the apex, lies just to the left of the sternum between the junction of the fourth and fifth ribs near their articulation with the costal cartilages. The right side of the heart is deflected anteriorly, and the left side is deflected posteriorly.
