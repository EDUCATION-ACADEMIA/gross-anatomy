GROSS ANATOMY

HEART MEDIASTINUM

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

All arteries carry blood away from the heart, while all veins carry blood to the heart.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Arteries carry blood away from the heart and veins carry blood back to the heart. The circulatory system carries oxygen, nutrients, and hormones to cells, and removes waste products, like carbon dioxide.

Concept note-2: -Arteries and veins link your heart to the rest of the circulatory system. Veins bring blood to your heart. Arteries take blood away from your heart. Your heart valves help control the direction the blood flows.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: The correct answer is true. Arteries carry oxygenated blood away from the heart, and veins carry deoxygenated blood toward the heart.

Concept note-4: -The arteries (red) carry oxygen and nutrients away from your heart, to your body’s tissues. The veins (blue) take oxygen-poor blood back to the heart. Arteries begin with the aorta, the large artery leaving the heart. They carry oxygen-rich blood away from the heart to all of the body’s tissues.

Concept note-5: -Arteries carry blood towards the heart and veins carry blood away from the heart to different organs. Q.
