Freshly oxygenated blood enters the heart through the ____, and is pumped out the ____

(A) right atrium; aorta

(B) ** left atrium; aorta

(C) right ventricle; pulmonary arteries

(D) left ventricle; pulmonary arteries

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Oxygen-rich blood then flows through the mitral valve (MV) into the left ventricle (LV), or the left lower chamber. The left ventricle (LV) pumps the oxygen-rich blood through the aortic valve (AoV) into the aorta (Ao), the main artery that takes oxygen-rich blood out to the rest of the body.

Concept note-2: -The oxygen-rich blood from the lungs then enters the left atrium and is pumped to the left ventricle. The left ventricle generates the high pressure needed to pump the blood to your whole body through your blood vessels.

Concept note-3: -On the left side Your mitral valve opens to send blood from your left atrium to your left ventricle. When your left ventricle is full it squeezes, which closes your mitral valve and opens your aortic valve. Your heart sends blood through your aortic valve to your aorta, where it flows to the rest of your body.

Concept note-4: -The heart pumps deoxygenated blood from the right ventricle to the lungs and at the same time pumps oxygenated blood from the left ventricle into the aorta.

Concept note-5: -Answer and Explanation: Freshly oxygenated blood is first received by the left atrium. When blood gets oxygenated from the lungs, it enters the left atrium through the pulmonary veins. Oxygenated blood then passes through the bicuspid valve and enters the left ventricle.
