HEART MEDIASTINUM

Which is the thickest?

(A) Veins

(B) Capillaries

(C) ** Arteries

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The arteries are the thickest of all blood vessels and have muscular walls that contract to keep the blood moving away from the heart. The veins are not as muscular, but they contain valves to prevent the blood from flowing backward. The arteries are therefore widest blood vessels.

Concept note-2: -They are the largest type of blood vessel in your body. The aorta is the largest artery in your body. The aorta carries blood from your heart to your organs.

Concept note-3: -The total wall thickness of arteries is around 1.0 mm and internal diameter of around 4.0 mm, with the exception of the Aorta, which has wall thickness of 2.0 mm and internal diameter of 25.0 mm. An artery is a blood vessel which carries blood away from the heart.

Concept note-4: -Compared to other blood vessels, arteries have thick walls and are located superficially.

Concept note-5: -Arteries experience a pressure wave as blood is pumped from the heart. This can be felt as a “pulse.” Because of this pressure the walls of arteries are much thicker than those of veins.
