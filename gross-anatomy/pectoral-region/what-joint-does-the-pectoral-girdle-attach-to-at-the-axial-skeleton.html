What joint does the pectoral girdle attach to at the axial skeleton?

(A) Acromioclavicular Joint

(B) ** Sternoclavicular Joint

(C) Glenohumeral Joint

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your clavicle provides the only direct connection between your pectoral girdle and axial skeleton.

Concept note-2: -The pectoral girdle is made up of two clavicles and two scapulae which are the anchors that attach the “appendicular” skeleton to the “axial” skeleton. There is only a single “true” anatomical joint between the pectoral girdle and the axial skeleton, and that is the sternoclavicular joint.

Concept note-3: -The pectoral girdle is a part of the appendicular skeleton, which connects the upper limbs to the axial skeleton. The human skeleton is divided into two sections, the axial skeleton and the appendicular skeleton. Axial skeleton is made up of 80 bones and includes the skull, the vertebral column, ribs and the sternum.

Concept note-4: -The shoulder, or pectoral, girdle is composed of the clavicles (collarbones) and the scapulae (shoulder blades). In humans the clavicles join the sternum (breastbone) medially and the scapulae laterally; the scapulae, however, are joined to the trunk only by muscles.

Concept note-5: -The clavicle connects the upper limb to the sternum (breast bone) by a joint, whereas the scapula is attached to the thoracic cage only by skeletal muscles.
