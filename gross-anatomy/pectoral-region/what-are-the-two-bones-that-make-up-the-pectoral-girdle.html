What are the two bones that make up the pectoral girdle?

(A) Radius & Ulna

(B) Tibia & Fibula

(C) Ilium & Ischuim

(D) ** Scapula & Clavicle

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The pectoral girdle is made up of two major bones: the clavicle and scapula.

Concept note-2: -Each half of the pectoral girdle consists of two bones, namely scapula (shoulder blade) and clavicle (collar bone).

Concept note-3: -The shoulder girdle or pectoral girdle is the set of bones in the appendicular skeleton which connects to the arm on each side. In humans it consists of the clavicle and scapula; in those species with three bones in the shoulder, it consists of the clavicle, scapula, and coracoid.

Concept note-4: -The medial end of the clavicle articulates with the manubrium of sternum (sternoclavicular joint), while the acromial end articulates with the acromion of the scapula (acromioclavicular joint).

Concept note-5: -A pair of shoulder girdles: These are named as pectoral girdles. This girdle articulates with the head of the humerus. A pair of hip girdles: These are named as pelvic girdles. These are located in the lower part of the trunk and articulate with the head of. femur. ulna.
