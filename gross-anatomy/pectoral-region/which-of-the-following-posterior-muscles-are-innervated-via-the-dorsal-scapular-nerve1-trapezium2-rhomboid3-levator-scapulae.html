Which of the following posterior muscles are innervated via the dorsal scapular nerve?1. Trapezium2. Rhomboid3. Levator Scapulae

(A) 1, 2, & 3

(B) ** 2 & 3

(C) 1 only

(D) 1 & 3

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Both rhomboids receive innervation from the dorsal scapular nerve and supplied by the dorsal scapular artery.

Concept note-2: -The DSN is a motor nerve that innervates the levator scapulae, rhomboid major, and rhomboid minor muscles. These muscles work dynamically and collectively are considered periscapular stabilizing muscles. Individually they can retract and elevate the scapula.

Concept note-3: -Dorsal Scapular Nerve is the branch of brachial plexus, arising from ventral ramus of C5 root. It is a motor nerve which arising from C5 root, pierce the middle scalene muscle, coursing deep to and supplying levator scapulae and the rhomboid muscles.

Concept note-4: -Both rhomboid muscles are innervated by the dorsal scapular nerve (C4-C5), a branch of the brachial plexus.

Concept note-5: -The scapula forms the posterior of the shoulder girdle. It is a sturdy, flat, triangular bone. The scapula provides attachment to several groups of muscles. The intrinsic muscles of the scapula include the rotator cuff muscles, teres major, subscapularis, teres minor, and infraspinatus.
