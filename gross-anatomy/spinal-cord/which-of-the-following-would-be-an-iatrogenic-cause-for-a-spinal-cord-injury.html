Which of the following would be an iatrogenic cause for a spinal cord injury?

(A) Neurosyphilis

(B) Meningitis

(C) Motor vehicle accident

(D) Osteoporosis

(E) ** Lumbar puncture

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A lumbar puncture may be done for various reasons. The most common reason is to remove a small amount of CSF for testing. This can help in the diagnosis of various disorders. The fluid is tested for red and white blood cells, protein, and glucose (sugar).

Concept note-2: -Side effects of a lumbar puncture headaches, which can last for up to a week – you’ll be given painkillers at the hospital if you need them. swelling and lower back pain where the needle was inserted – this should get better on its own after a few days and is normally nothing to worry about.

Concept note-3: -Post-LP headache-Headache, which occurs in 10 to 30 percent of patients, is one of the most common complications following LP. Post-LP headache is caused by leakage of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) from the dura and traction on pain-sensitive structures.

Concept note-4: -Introduction. Iatrogenic epidermoid cysts can arise as a late complication of lumbar puncture. These rare tumors are slow-growing and typically present years after the initiating event. Because symptoms are nonspecific, diagnosis is aided by a comprehensive history, imaging, and histopathology.
