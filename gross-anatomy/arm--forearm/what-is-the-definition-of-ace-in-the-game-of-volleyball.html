What is the definition of Ace in the game of Volleyball?

(A) The person who is the team leader.

(B) A defensive strategy that relies on quickly moving to the side line and stopping.

(C) ** A serve that cannot be returned and therefore the team receives a point.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The term “ace” refers to when a player serves the ball and the opposing team is unable to pass it. An ace occurs when the ball either hits the ground or is shanked off of a passer making a second touch impossible.

Concept note-2: -Ace: A serve that is not returned and results in a point. Assist: Passing or setting the ball to a player who attacks the ball for a point. Block: A defensive play, where a player deflects a spiked ball back into the opponent’s court by jumping in front of the ball with their hands above the net.

Concept note-3: -If a serve lands in the service box and the returner is unable to touch it with their racket, it is called an ‘ace’. This wins the point for the server. An ace does not have to be fast: spin and deception can also defeat the returner.

Concept note-4: -Ace: A serve that the receiving team cannot put into play. Attack: The act of forcefully sending the ball to an opponent in a forceful manner: often called a spike. Base Formation: A team’s ready position while its opponents play the ball.

Concept note-5: -What counts as an ace in volleyball? In volleyball, an ace is a serve that directly results in a point. If you serve the ball and the other team really isn’t able to handle it, that’s an ace! It’s the most common way that you as a player can score a point for your team all by yourself.
