EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Most labs say that a normal serum TSH level is somewhere between 0.4 to 4.0 mIU/L, and that a TSH level of 10 mIU/L or higher is indicative of hypothyroidism. A TSH level of 4.5 to 10 mIU/L is considered indicative of subclinical hypothyroidism.

Concept note-2: -High thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) levels based on a TSH test may mean you have hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid), while low TSH levels suggest hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid). TSH values are described as follows:1. Normal: Between 0.4 and 4.0 mU/L. High: Over 4.5 mU/L. Low: Under 0.4 mu/L.

Concept note-3: -A normal range for TSH in most laboratories is 0.4 milliunits per liter (mU/L) to 4.0 mU/L. If your TSH is higher than 4.0 mU/L on repeat tests, you probably have hypothyroidism. Concept note-4: -The normal range of TSH levels in non-pregnant adult women is 0.5 to 5.0 mIU/L. A TSH level higher than 5.0 usually indicates an underactive thyroid (hypothyroidism) and a TSH level lower than 0.4 indicates the presence of excessive thyroid hormone and overactive thyroid (hyperthyroidism).
