a dermatome is an area of ____ supplied by a ____ ____

(A) ** a dermatome is an area of SKIN supplied by a NERVE ROOT

(B) a dermatome is an area of SKIN supplied by a PERIPHERAL NERVE

(C) a dermatome is an area of MUSCLE supplied by a NERVE ROOT

(D) a dermatome is an area of SKIN supplied by a NERVE INJURY

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A dermatome is an area of skin supplied by a single spinal nerve. There are 31 pairs of spinal nerves, forming nerve roots that branch from your spinal cord. Your spinal nerves help to relay sensory, motor, and autonomic information between the rest of your body and your central nervous system (CNS).

Concept note-2: -A dermatome is an area of skin that is mainly supplied by a single spinal nerve. There are 8 cervical nerves (note C1 has with no dermatome), 12 thoracic nerves, 5 lumbar nerves and 5 sacral nerves.

Concept note-3: -Dermatomes are derived from the outer portion of an embryo from which the skin and subcutaneous tissues are developed and become the areas of skin supplied by the branches of a single dorsal root ganglion.

Concept note-4: -Dermatomes are areas of skin on your body that rely on specific nerve connections on your spine. In this way, dermatomes are much like a map. The nature of that connection means that dermatomes can help a healthcare provider detect and diagnose conditions or problems affecting your spine, spinal cord or spinal nerves.

Concept note-5: -Each dermatome is a region of the skin that is innervated by a single spinal nerve. Dermatomes are segregated across the body and can vary from one person to another. These regions help in diagnosing problems associated with a particular nerve root within the spine.
