BRACHIAL PLEXUS

Which lists the proximal to distal progression of the brachial plexus in the right order?

(A) ** Roots, Trunks, Divisions, Chords, Branches

(B) Roots, Divisions, Cords, Trunks, Branches

(C) Branches, Chords, Divisions, Trunks, Roots

(D) Roots, Branches, Divisions, Chords, Trunks

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The brachial plexus is divided, proximally to distally into rami/roots, trunks, divisions, cords, and terminal branches. The trunks can be found within the posterior triangle of the neck, between the anterior and middle scalene muscles.

Concept note-2: -The radial nerve is the second terminal branch of the posterior cord of the brachial plexus, that contains fibers of spinal nerves C5-T1. This nerve is a mixed nerve that provides motor and sensory innervation to the arm and forearm.

Concept note-3: -Brachial Plexus Anatomy Trunks The C5-T1 brachial plexus roots form three trunks as they travel across the posterior triangle of the neck. Superior trunk – C5 and C6 roots. Middle trunk – C7 root. Inferior trunk – C8 and T1 roots.

Concept note-4: -Shortly after emerging from the intervertebral foramina, the 5 roots (C5-T1) unite to form 3 trunks. The trunks of the brachial plexus pass between the anterior and middle scalene muscles. The ventral rami of C5 and C6 unite to form the upper trunk.

Concept note-5: -Structure. The brachial plexus is divided into five roots, three trunks, six divisions (three anterior and three posterior), three cords, and five branches.
