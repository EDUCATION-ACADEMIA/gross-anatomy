GROSS ANATOMY

AUTONOMIC NERVOUS SYSTEM

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

The ____ nervous system controls unconscious activities.

(A) somatic

(B) sympathetic

(C) parasympathetic

(D) ** autonomic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your autonomic nervous system is a network of nerves throughout your body that control unconscious processes. These are things that happen without you thinking about them, such as breathing and your heart beating.

Concept note-2: -The autonomic nervous system is a component of the peripheral nervous system that regulates involuntary physiologic processes including heart rate, blood pressure, respiration, digestion, and sexual arousal. It contains three anatomically distinct divisions: sympathetic, parasympathetic, and enteric.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: The organs that are not controlled by the autonomic nervous system are the skeletal muscles. Unlike the organs of the autonomic nervous system, such as the heart, kidneys, and liver, the skeletal muscles are under conscious control.

Concept note-4: -The autonomic nervous system Autonomic nervous system is the part of the nervous system that supplies the internal organs, including the blood vessels, stomach, intestine, liver, kidneys, bladder, genitals, lungs, pupils, heart, and sweat, salivary, and digestive glands.

Concept note-5: -The autonomic nervous system (ANS) is also called the vegetative nervous system. It controls the involuntary functions and influences the activity of internal organs.
