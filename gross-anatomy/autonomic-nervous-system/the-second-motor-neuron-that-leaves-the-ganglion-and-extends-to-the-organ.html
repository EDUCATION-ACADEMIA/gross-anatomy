AUTONOMIC NERVOUS SYSTEM

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

The second motor neuron that leaves the ganglion and extends to the organ

(A) cholinergic neuron

(B) ** postganglionic neuron

(C) preganglionic neuron

(D) adrenergic neuron

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The second motor neuron is called a ganglionic neuron. The cell body of the ganglionic neuron resides within the autonomic ganglion and its axon extends to an effector (cardiac muscle, smooth muscle, or gland) forming a postganglionic fiber.

Concept note-2: -The cell body of the second neuron (also in the autonomic ganglion) its unmyelinanted axon extends from the ganglion to the effector (smooth muscle, cardiac, or gland).

Concept note-3: -Answer: Autonomic Nervous System This part of the autonomic nervous system involves the cranial nerves of the brain as well as sacral nerves.

Concept note-4: -The postganglionic neuron, the second neuron in the autonomic motor pathway, lies entirely outside the CNS. a. Its cell body and dendrites are located in an autonomic ganglion, where it makes synapses with one or more preganglionic fibers.

Concept note-5: -Nicotinic receptors are found on the cell bodies of all postganglionic neurons, both sympathetic and parasympathetic, in the ganglia of the ANS.
