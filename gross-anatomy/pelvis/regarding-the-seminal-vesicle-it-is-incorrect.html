Regarding the seminal vesicle, it is INCORRECT:

(A) Shape: flattened and embossed

(B) Length: 5 to 6 cm

(C) ** Produces an acidic liquid rich in fructose

(D) Produces alkaline liquid rich in fructose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Fructose is secreted from the seminal vesicles and the accessory sex glands. It is the major carbohydrate source in seminal plasma and essential for normal sperm motility.

Concept note-2: -The fluid from the seminal vesicles is viscous and contains fructose, which provides an energy source for the sperm; prostaglandins, which contribute to the mobility and viability of the sperm; and proteins that cause slight coagulation reactions in the semen after ejaculation.

Concept note-3: -The seminal vesicles make a sugar-rich fluid (fructose) that provides sperm with a source of energy and helps with the sperms’ ability to move (motility). The fluid of the seminal vesicles makes up most of the volume of your ejaculatory fluid, or ejaculate.

Concept note-4: -The seminal vesicles produce a fluid characterized by a high content of fructose, prostaglandins, and semenogelins (17–19).
