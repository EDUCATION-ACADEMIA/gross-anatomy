GROSS ANATOMY

ORBIT EYE

[SOURCES]
It is the small opening in the middle of the iris. It allows the the light rays to enter the eye.

(A) ** Pupil

(B) Iris

(C) Lens

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Pupil: The pupil is the opening at the center of the iris through which light passes. The iris adjusts the size of the pupil to control the amount of light that enters the eye. Retina: The retina is the light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye.

Concept note-2: -The pupil is the hole located in the center of the iris. It allows light to enter the eye. The pupil appears black because light rays entering the pupil are absorbed by the tissues inside the eye. Or they are absorbed after diffused reflections within the eye. The iris is a thin, circular structure in the eye.

Concept note-3: -Iris regulates the amount of light entering the eye by adjusting its size. Iris is a thin membrane, which controls pupil which in turn regulates the amount of light entering the eye.

Concept note-4: -The iris controls the amount of light that enters the eye by opening and closing the pupil. The iris uses muscles to change the size of the pupil. These muscles can control the amount of light entering the eye by making the pupil larger (dilated) or smaller (constricted).

Concept note-5: -The round opening in the center of the iris (the colored tissue that makes the “eye color” at the front of the eye). The pupil changes size to let light into the eye. It gets smaller in bright light and larger as the amount of light decreases.
