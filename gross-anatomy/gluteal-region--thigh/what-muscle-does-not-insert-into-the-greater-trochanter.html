What muscle does not insert into the greater trochanter

(A) The piriformis

(B) The glutues medius

(C) The superior gemellus

(D) ** The quadratus femoris

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The gluteus maximus does not insert on the greater trochanter. It inserts on the gluteal tuberosity and lateral femur. The gluteus medius inserts on the lateral surface of the greater trochanter.

Concept note-2: -The greater trochanter is the attachment site for five muscles: the gluteus medius, gluteus minimus, piriformis, obturator externus, and obturator internus.

Concept note-3: -The vastus lateralis is the largest muscle in the quadriceps. The vastus lateralis originates from the greater trochanter and is found on the lateral aspect of the thigh.

Concept note-4: -The quadratus femoris is a flat, quadrilateral skeletal muscle. Located on the posterior side of the hip joint, it is a strong external rotator and adductor of the thigh, but also acts to stabilize the femoral head in the acetabulum.
