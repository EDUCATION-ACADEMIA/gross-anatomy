GLUTEAL REGION THIGH

[SOURCES]
Which of the following muscles laterally rotates the iliofemoral joint? (select all that apply)

(A) ** gemellus inferior

(B) semitendinosus

(C) ** piriformis

(D) gluteus minimus

(E) ** gluteus medius

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lateral rotation: Primarily accomplished by the obturator muscles, the quadratus femoris, and the gemelli with assistance from the gluteus maximus, sartorius, and piriformis. Abduction: Primarily accomplished by the gluteus medius and minimus with assistance from the tensor fascia latae and sartorius.

Concept note-2: -Actions of Gluteus Medius on the thigh (femur): Medially Rotate thigh at the hip. Laterally Rotate thigh at the hip.

Concept note-3: -The piriformis is a long muscle that originates on the anterior surface of the lateral process of the sacrum and gluteal surface of the ilium at the margin of the greater sciatic notch. It inserts on the superior border of the greater trochanter. Like the gemellus muscles, the piriformis rotates the hip laterally.

Concept note-4: -The sartorius, pectineus, obturator internus and externus, both gemelli, the gluteal group (maximus, medius, minimus), piriformis and quadratus femoris are the most significant contributors to lateral rotation (Visible Body 2019).

Concept note-5: -The anterior fibers also flex and medially rotate the thigh and anteriorly tilt and ipsilaterally rotate the pelvis at the hip joint. The posterior fibers also extend and laterally rotate the thigh and posteriorly tilt and contralaterally rotate the pelvis at the hip joint.
