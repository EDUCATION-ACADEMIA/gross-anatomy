I of iliacus

(A) ** With psoas major on lesser trochanter

(B) With psoas major on greater trochanter

(C) With psoas major on medial trochanter

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The iliacus originates in the iliac fossa of the pelvis. The psoas major unites with the iliacus at the level of the inguinal ligament. It crosses the hip joint to insert on the lesser trochanter of the femur. The iliopsoas is classified as an “anterior hip muscle” or “inner hip muscle".

Concept note-2: -The psoas muscle is among the most significant muscles that overlie the vertebral column. It is a long fusiform muscle on either side of the vertebral column and the brim of the lesser pelvis. At its distal end, it combines with the iliacus muscle to form the iliopsoas muscle.

Concept note-3: -Psoas major is often considered with the iliacus muscle. It merges with iliacus as it passes inferolaterally into the thigh to insert onto the femur. Together, these muscles are known as the iliopsoas muscle.
