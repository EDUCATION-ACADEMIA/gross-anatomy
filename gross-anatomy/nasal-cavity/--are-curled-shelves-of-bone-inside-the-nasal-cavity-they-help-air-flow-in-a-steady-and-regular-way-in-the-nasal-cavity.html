NASAL CAVITY

* ____ are curled shelves of bone inside the nasal cavity. They help air flow in a steady and regular way in the nasal cavity.

(A) nasal cavity

(B) ** nasal conchae

(C) nasal receptors

(D) olfactory epithellium

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In humans, the conchae divide the nasal airway into four groove-like air passages, and are responsible for forcing inhaled air to flow in a steady, regular pattern around the largest possible surface area of nasal mucosa.

Concept note-2: -The superior and middle nasal conchae are processes of the ethmoid bone, while the inferior nasal concha is formed by a separate cranial bone (of the same name).

Concept note-3: -nasal concha, also called Turbinate, or Turbinal, any of several thin, scroll-shaped bony elements forming the upper chambers of the nasal cavities. They increase the surface area of these cavities, thus providing for rapid warming and humidification of air as it passes to the lungs.

Concept note-4: -The inferior nasal concha is a scroll-shaped, paired bone that rests at the lateral wall of the nasal cavity. It helps to filter and humidify the air that we breathe. The nasal conchae (plural of concha) are also sometimes called turbinates.

Concept note-5: -turbinates (turbinate bones or nasal conchae) are thin, curved, bony plates that project from the walls of the nasal cavity into the respiratory passageway.
