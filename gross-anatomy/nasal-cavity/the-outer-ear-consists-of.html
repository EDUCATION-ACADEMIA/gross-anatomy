The outer ear consists of

(A) eardrum, oval window, ossicles, eustachian tube

(B) eardrum, ossicles, eustachian tube

(C) ** pinna, ear canal

(D) semicircular canals, cochlea, auditory nerves

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The outer ear consists of the visible portion on the side of the head, known as the pinna [1], and the external auditory canal (ear canal) [2]. The purpose of the pinna is to catch sound waves, amplify them slightly, and funnel them down the ear canal to the tympanic membrane (eardrum) [3].

Concept note-2: -The outer ear is made up of cartilage and skin. There are three different parts to the outer ear; the tragus, helix and the lobule. The ear canal starts at the outer ear and ends at the ear drum.

Concept note-3: -Outer part of ear consists of pinna, auditory canal and ear drum.

Concept note-4: -The auricle (pinna) is the visible portion of the outer ear. It collects sound waves and channels them into the ear canal (external auditory meatus), where the sound is amplified. The sound waves then travel toward a flexible, oval membrane at the end of the ear canal called the eardrum, or tympanic membrane.

Concept note-5: -The ear canal, also called the external acoustic meatus, is a passage comprised of bone and skin leading to the eardrum. The ear is comprised of the ear canal (also known as the outer ear), the middle ear, and the inner ear.
