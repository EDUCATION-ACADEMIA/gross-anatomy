Openings where perspiration or sweat comes out.

(A) dermis

(B) epidermis

(C) ** pores

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Eccrine sweat glands occur over most of the body and open directly onto the skin’s surface. Apocrine glands open into the hair follicle, leading to the surface of the skin. Apocrine glands develop in areas with many hair follicles, such as on the scalp, armpits and groin.

Concept note-2: -The distal or apical part of the duct that opens to the skin’s surface is known as the acrosyringium. Each sweat gland receives several nerve fibers that branch out into bands of one or more axons and encircle the individual tubules of the secretory coil.

Concept note-3: -The sweat leaves your skin through tiny holes called pores.

Concept note-4: -Sweat is produced by glands in the deeper layer of the skin, the dermis. Sweat glands occur all over the body, but are most numerous on the forehead, the armpits, the palms and the soles of the feet. Sweat is mainly water, but it also contains some salts. Its main function is to control body temperature.
