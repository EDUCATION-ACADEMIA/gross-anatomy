Nose hairs ____

(A) are gross

(B) do not help with anything

(C) ** capture dust, germs, and other small particles

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The mucous membrane makes mucus, that sticky stuff in your nose you might call snot. Mucus captures dust, germs, and other small particles that could irritate your lungs. If you look inside your nose, you will also see hairs that can trap large particles, like dirt or pollen.

Concept note-2: -Nostrils have fine hair to trap dust particles present in the air and stops them from entering our respiratory system. The nostrils are separated by the septum.

Concept note-3: -∙ Mucus and the hair inside the nose trap the dust particles and germs present in the inhaled air.

Concept note-4: -Nasal hair is the hair inside the nose. It is one of the body’s first line of defence against harmful environmental pathogens such as germs, fungus, and spores that are inhaled. Another purpose of nasal hair is to provide additional humidity to the inhaled air.

Concept note-5: -The hair and mucus that are present inside the nose filter the air that goes into the lungs. That is, these stop the germs and dust from entering into our body.
