GROSS ANATOMY

THORACIC WALL BREAST

[SOURCES]
This is the passageway for tears:

(A) ** lacrimals

(B) vomer

(C) palatines

(D) inferior nasal conchae

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The lacrimal duct system transmits tears from the surface of the eye to the nasal cavity. Tears enter the duct system at the lacrimal punctae and conduct through canaliculi within the eyelids. The canaliculi drain into the lacrimal sac.

Concept note-2: -Tears are produced by the lacrimal gland and the accessory lacrimal glands and cover the cornea. Tears then drain out the punctum in the medial eyelids, flow down the canaliculi into the lacrimal sac. They then drain down the nasolacrimal duct into the nose at the inferior meatus (under the inferior turbinate).

Concept note-3: -Pathway of lacrimal fluid At the medial canthal region of the eye, the fluid collects in a triangular space called the lacrimal lake. The fluid is subsequently drained by capillary action into the lacrimal canaliculi which are small channels within each eyelid, via the lacrimal puncta.

Concept note-4: -Tears are drained down from the conjunctival sac through lacrimal pathways to the inferior nasal meatus and then to the nasopharynx. The entrance of the lacrimal pathways is formed by 2 small (0.2â€“0.3 mm in diameter) orifices, known as the upper and lower lacrimal puncta.

Concept note-5: -Tears drain into openings, called puncta, on the inside corners of your upper and lower eyelids. The puncta lead to small canals called canaliculi. Canaliculi move tears to a sac to a reservoir on the side of the nose called the lacrimal sac. From there tears travel down the nasolacrimal duct and drain into your nose.
