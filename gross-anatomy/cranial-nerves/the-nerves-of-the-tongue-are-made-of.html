CRANIAL NERVES

The nerves of the tongue are made of:

(A) Sensory fibers

(B) ** Motor fibers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The hypoglossal nerve (CN XII) provides motor innervation to all of the intrinsic and extrinsic muscles of the tongue except for the palatoglossus muscle, which is innervated by the vagus nerve (CN X).

Concept note-2: -The hypoglossal nerve is mainly a somatic efferent (motor) nerve to innervate the tongue musculature. The nerve also contains some sympathetic postganglionic fibers from the cervical ganglia, which innervates tongue vessels and some small glands in the oral mucosa.

Concept note-3: -Nerve supply Innervation of the tongue consists of motor fibers, special sensory fibers for taste, and general sensory fibers for sensation.

Concept note-4: -The tongue is a muscular organ situated in the oral cavity, and an accessory digestive organ. Its main functions include sensation of taste, mastication (chewing), deglutition (swallowing), speech, and clearing the oral cavity.

Concept note-5: -Lingual nerve (LN) is divided into 6 to 7 main branches outside the tongue. Two or three anterior LN branches with numerous twigs travel anteriorly to the tongue tip. Three middle LN branches pass through the tongue muscles to reach the dorsal mucosa.
