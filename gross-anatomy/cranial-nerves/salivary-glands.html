[SOURCES]
Salivary glands

(A) ** facial

(B) optic

(C) olfactory

(D) accessory

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your parotid glands are major salivary glands. You have two of them, and theyâ€™re located just in front of and below each ear. Like your other major salivary glands (submandibular and sublingual), your parotid glands produce saliva (spit) to keep your mouth lubricated, and to aid in chewing and digestion.

Concept note-2: -You have three pairs of major salivary glands under and behind your jaw-parotid, sublingual and submandibular. Many other tiny salivary glands are in your lips, inside your cheeks, and throughout your mouth and throat.

Concept note-3: -Visceromotor function. The parasympathetic component of the facial nerve is important. It controls the lacrimal, submandibular and sublingual salivary glands, as well as the pharyngeal, nasal and palatine glands.

Concept note-4: -In the oral mucosa, there are plenty of minor salivary glands including the inner aspect of the lips. Each gland has its own duct draining into the oral cavity.

Concept note-5: -Sucking on citrus fruits or hard candies. Sucking on a wedge of lemon or orange increases the flow of saliva, which can help dislodge the stone. Drinking plenty of fluids. Gentle massage. Medications. Sucking on ice cubes. 12-Feb-2019
