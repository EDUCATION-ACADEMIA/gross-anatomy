GROSS ANATOMY

CRANIAL NERVES

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

Ask patient to hold their mouth open while you try to close it; with patients eyes closed, touch face with cotton and have them identify which area is being touched; touch cornea with cotton.

(A) CN I

(B) CN VIII

(C) CN IX

(D) ** CN V

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cranial nerve I (olfactory nerve). This is the nerve of smell. The patient may be asked to identify different smells with his or her eyes closed.

Concept note-2: -The trigeminal nerve is one of the 12 cranial nerves. It can transmit both sensory and motor information to the brain. The trigeminal nerve branches out to different parts of the face to communicate sensory information like temperature, touch, and pain. It also stimulates movement in the jaw muscles.

Concept note-3: -Cranial Nerve VII – Facial Nerve Ask the patient to smile, show teeth, close both eyes, puff cheeks, frown, and raise eyebrows. Look for symmetry and strength of facial muscles.

Concept note-4: -The 12th (hypoglossal) cranial nerve is evaluated by asking the patient to extend the tongue and inspecting it for atrophy, fasciculations, and weakness (deviation is toward the side of a lesion).
