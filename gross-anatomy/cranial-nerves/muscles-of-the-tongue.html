Muscles of the tongue:

(A) Facial (VII)

(B) ** Hypoglossal (XII)

(C) Accessory (XI)

(D) Vagus (X)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Genioglossus muscles, which push the tongue forward. Hyoglossus muscles that pull the tongue back and flatten it. Intrinsic muscles that change the tongueâ€™s shape, including curving and narrowing. Styloglossus muscles, which move the tongue up and down. 04-Jun-2021

Concept note-2: -The hypoglossal nerve innervates all the intrinsic muscles and all but one of the extrinsic muscles (genioglossus, styloglossus, and hyoglossus) of the tongue.

Concept note-3: -Disorders of the 12th cranial nerve (hypoglossal nerve) cause weakness or wasting (atrophy) of the tongue on the affected side. This nerve moves the tongue. Hypoglossal nerve disorders may be caused by tumors, strokes, infections, injuries, or amyotrophic lateral sclerosis.

Concept note-4: -Results: Twelfth-nerve palsies usually appear as signs rather than symptoms. Tumors, predominantly malignant, produced nearly half of the palsies (49 cases), while gunshot wounds made trauma (12) the second most common cause.

Concept note-5: -This nerve controls all tongue movements. Nuclear or infranuclear lesions produce paralysis, atrophy, and fasciculations of the tongue on the involved side.
