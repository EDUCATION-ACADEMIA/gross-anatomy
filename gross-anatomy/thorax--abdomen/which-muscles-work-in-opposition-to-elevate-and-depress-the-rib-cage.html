Which muscles work in opposition to elevate and depress the rib cage?

(A) Serratus Anterior & Serratus Posterior Superior

(B) ** Serratus Posterior Superior & Serratus Posterior Inferior

(C) Serratus Anterior & Serratus Posterior Inferior

(D) Serratus Posterior Superior & Rhomboid Major

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Intermediate back muscles-work to elevate and depress the rib cage. There are two major muscles within this category â€“ the serratus posterior superior and the serratus posterior inferior. Back muscles-Includes Erector Spinae and Transversospinalis Group (intrinsic back muscles).

Concept note-2: -Serratus posterior superior: elevate the ribs during inspiration(accessory muscle of inspiration) and has a stabilization role for thorax.

Concept note-3: -The serratus posterior muscles extend obliquely from the vertebral column to the rib cage. The main function of these muscles is to facilitate the act of respiration; the serratus posterior superior muscle elevates the ribs, while the serratus posterior inferior muscle depresses the ribs.

Concept note-4: -Another function of the serratus anterior is the active stabilization of the scapula within the shoulder. Finally, in a fixed scapula the muscle lifts the ribs and acts as an accessory inspiratory muscle.

Concept note-5: -The serratus posterior superior and inferior muscles are generally considered clinically insignificant muscles that, based on attachments, probably function in respiration.
