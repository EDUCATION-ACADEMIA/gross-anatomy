THORAX ABDOMEN

Muscle that separates the thorax and the abdominal cavity

(A) ** diaphragm

(B) esophagus

(C) trachea

(D) larynx

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The diaphragm is a thin dome-shaped muscle which separates the thoracic cavity (lungs and heart) from the abdominal cavity (intestines, stomach, liver, etc.). It is involved in respiration, drawing downward in the chest on inhalation, and pushing upward in exhalation.

Concept note-2: -The diaphragm is an important muscle for respiratory function. It forms the inferior border of the thoracic cavity separating the abdomen from the thorax.

Concept note-3: -The thoracic cavity is separated from the abdominopelvic cavity by the diaphragm. The thoracic cavity is further separated into the pleural cavity which contains the lungs and the superior mediastinum which includes the pericardial (heart) cavity. The organs within the ventral body cavity are called the viscera.

Concept note-4: -Pelvis. The abdominal and pelvic cavities are bounded by the respiratory diaphragm cranially (superiorly), the inferior ribs and bony pelvis laterally, and the pelvic diaphragm caudally (inferiorly) (Figure 4.12E, F).

Concept note-5: -The diaphragm is a sheet of skeletal muscle that separates the thoracic cavity from the abdominal cavity.
