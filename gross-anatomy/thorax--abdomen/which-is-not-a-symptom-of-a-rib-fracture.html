Which is not a symptom of a rib fracture?

(A) ** deviated trachea

(B) swelling

(C) difficulty breathing

(D) pain worse when inhaling

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Overview. If you’ve ever had a rib fracture-a crack or an actual break in a rib-you know that this type of injury can cause severe pain. A hallmark of a rib fracture is that a deep breath makes the pain worse. Pain from a fractured rib can even impede your breathing, leading to a chest infection.

Concept note-2: -Non-displaced fractures are still broken bones, but the pieces weren’t moved far enough to be out of alignment during the break. Displaced fractures are much more likely to require surgery to repair.

Concept note-3: -strong pain in your chest area, particularly when you breathe in. swelling or tenderness around the affected ribs. sometimes bruising on the skin. feeling or hearing a crack if it’s a broken rib.

Concept note-4: -pain in the chest that gets worse when laughing, coughing or sneezing. pain when breathing in. difficulty breathing. tenderness to the chest or back over the ribs. bruising. swelling. a ‘crunchy’ or ‘crackling’ feeling under the skin or in the ribs. coughing up blood. 19-Dec-2017
