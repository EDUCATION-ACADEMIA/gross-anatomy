LUMBOSACRAL PLEXUS

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

Ilio-inguinal levels in the spinal roots:

(A) ** L1-L4

(B) L2-L5

(C) L4-S1

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your lumbar spine consists of the five bones (vertebra) in your lower back. Your lumbar vertebrae, known as L1 to L5, are the largest of your entire spine. Your lumbar spine is located below your 12 chest (thoracic) vertebra and above the five fused bones that make up your triangular-shaped sacrum bone.

Concept note-2: -The ilioinguinal nerve is a continuation of the anterior ramus of spinal nerve L1. In some cases, it may also receive contributions from the subcostal (T12) nerve or L2. Upon its origin, the nerve passes posterior to the psoas major and then emerges to the anterior surface of the quadratus lumborum.

Concept note-3: -L2, L3, and L4 spinal nerves provide sensation to the front part of the thigh and inner side of the lower leg. These nerves also control movements of the hip and knee muscles.

Concept note-4: -The lumbar plexus originates from the anterior rami of spinal nerves L1-L4 and is formed largely within the posterior aspect of the psoas major muscle. The anterior ramus of spinal nerve T12 contributes to the formation of the lumbar plexus via the dorsolumbar nerve, which joins the anterior ramus of spinal nerve L1.
