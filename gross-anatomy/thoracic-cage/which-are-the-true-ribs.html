Which are the true ribs?

(A) ** Pairs #1 to #7

(B) Pairs #1 to #10

(C) Pairs #8 to #10

(D) Pairs #11 and #12

(E) Pairs #13 and #14

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In humans there are normally 12 pairs of ribs. The first seven pairs are attached directly to the sternum by costal cartilages and are called true ribs.

Concept note-2: -There are seven pairs of true ribs. They are the most superior of the thoracic ribs. They are sometimes called vertebrosternal ribs. They differ from false and floating ribs because they directly articulate with the sternum by means of their costal cartilages.

Concept note-3: -The ribs are anchored posteriorly to the 12 thoracic vertebrae. The sternum consists of the manubrium, body, and xiphoid process. The ribs are classified as true ribs (1–7) and false ribs (8–12). The last two pairs of false ribs are also known as floating ribs (11–12).

Concept note-4: -The first seven bones are called the true ribs. These bones are connected to the spine (the backbone) in back. In the front, the true ribs are connected directly to the breastbone or sternum by a strips of cartilage called the costal cartilage. The next three pairs of bones are called false ribs.

Concept note-5: -The 8th, 9th, and 10th pairs-false ribs-do not join the sternum directly but are connected to the 7th rib by cartilage.
