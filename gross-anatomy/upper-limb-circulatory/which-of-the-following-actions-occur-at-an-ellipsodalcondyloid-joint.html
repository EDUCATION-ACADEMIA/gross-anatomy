Which of the following actions occur at an ellipsodal/condyloid joint?

(A) flexion/extension only

(B) pronation/supination only

(C) ** abduction/adduction, flexion/extension and circumduction

(D) abduction/adduction, flexion/extension, medial rotation/lateral rotation and circumduction

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The ellipsoid joint permits the movement of the bone in two planes, allowing flexion, extension, adduction, abduction, and circumduction. The ellipsoid joints occur between the metacarpals and phalanges.

Concept note-2: -Condyloid joints, also known as ellipsoid joints, are composed of an egg-shaped bone known as a condyle that fits into a similarly shaped cavity. Although it sounds similar to a ball and socket joint, condyloid joints only allow for forward-backward and side to side movement and do not allow rotation.

Concept note-3: -An ellipsoid joint allows movements in all angular motions. The movement of ellipsoid joints is in two plains, back and front, and side to side. These joints are present between the knuckle joints, wrist joints, metacarpophalangeal joints and metatarsophalangeal joints of fingers.

Concept note-4: -A condyloid joint is a modified ball and socket joint that allows for flexion, extension, abduction, and adduction movements.

Concept note-5: -Final answer: The wrist joint, the metacarpophalangeal joints, and the metatarsophalangeal joints are examples of ellipsoid joints.
