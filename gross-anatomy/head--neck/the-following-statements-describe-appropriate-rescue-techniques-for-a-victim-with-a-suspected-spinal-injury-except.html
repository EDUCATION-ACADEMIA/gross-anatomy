The following statements describe appropriate rescue techniques for a victim with a suspected spinal injury, EXCEPT

(A) If the victim is in shallow water, you do not need to use a rescue tube to support yourself

(B) If the victim is submerged, you should not use the rescue tube when submerging and bringing the victim to the surface

(C) ** If the victim is small and is in shallow water, you do not need to use a backboard to extricate the victim.

(D) If the victim is at the surface in deep water, you may need a rescue tube to support yourself and the victim

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Remove the victim from the water using the spinal back boarding procedure. The following statements describe appropriate rescue techniques for a victim with a suspected spinal injury, EXCEPT: If the victim is small and is in shallow water, you do not need to use a backboard to extricate the victim.

Concept note-2: -If you suspect someone has a spinal injury: Call 911 or emergency medical help. Keep the person still. Place heavy towels or rolled sheets on both sides of the neck or hold the head and neck to prevent movement. Avoid moving the head or neck.

Concept note-3: -The head splint technique is used for performing manual in-line stabilization for victims in the water (Figure 11-1). You can use this technique when the victim is face-up or face-down.

Concept note-4: -If the victim is face down, roll the victim face up as they are brought to the surface. If the victim is suspected of having a spinal cord injury, follow the backboarding steps to remove the victim from the water.
