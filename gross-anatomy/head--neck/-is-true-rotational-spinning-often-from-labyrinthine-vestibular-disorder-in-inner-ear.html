HEAD NECK

[SOURCES]
____ is true rotational spinning often from labyrinthine-vestibular disorder in inner ear.

(A) Dizziness

(B) Nausea

(C) Syncope

(D) ** Vertigo

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Vertigo is caused by a problem with the nerves and structures in the inner ear that control balance (vestibular labyrinth). Benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV) occurs when tiny canalith particles (otoconia) break loose and fall into the wrong part of the semicircular canals of the inner ear.

Concept note-2: -A sense that you or your surroundings are spinning or moving (vertigo) A loss of balance or unsteadiness.

Concept note-3: -What is the difference between vertigo and labyrinthitis? Labyrinthitis is inflammation of the inner ear labyrinth. Vertigo, a common symptom of labyrinthitis, causes a person to feel as though their surroundings are spinning.

Concept note-4: -Vertigo has a rotational, spinning component, and is the perception of movement, either of the self or surrounding objects. Disequilibrium refers to unsteadiness, imbalance, or loss of equilibrium that is often accompanied by spatial disorientation.
