which is a sign of an injured ear?

(A) hearing loss

(B) loss of balance

(C) inner ear pain

(D) ** all of these seem correct

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ear injuries can range from minor to life-threatening. If you or your child has severe ear pain, bleeding ears, dizziness or hearing loss, get help right away. These are signs of a serious medical condition, especially after a blow to the head, fall or other accident.

Concept note-2: -These ear injuries usually happen from a blow to your head or an object going inside your ear. Ossicular dislocation and ossicular fracture cause hearing loss, but treatment can help. A temporal bone fracture (break) happens from a blow to your head and can be a life-threatening injury.

Concept note-3: -a very high temperature or feel hot and shivery. earache that does not start to get better after 3 days. swelling around the ear. fluid coming from the ear. hearing loss or a change in hearing. other symptoms, like being sick, a severe sore throat or dizziness. regular ear infections. More items

Concept note-4: -Cuts, scrapes, burns, or frostbite. Inserting something into the ear. Direct blows to the ear or head. Loud noise. Sudden air pressure changes. Vestibular therapy can help kids who have balance problems.
