HEAD NECK

[SOURCES]
Which artery supplies blood to the head

(A) Jugular vein

(B) ** Carotid artery

(C) Radial artery

(D) Femoral artery

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Carotid Artery. Your carotid arteries supply blood and oxygen to your brain.

Concept note-2: -The brain receives blood from two sources: the internal carotid arteries, which arise at the point in the neck where the common carotid arteries bifurcate, and the vertebral arteries (Figure 1.20). The internal carotid arteries branch to form two major cerebral arteries, the anterior and middle cerebral arteries.

Concept note-3: -The carotid arteries are a pair of blood vessels located on the sides of your neck that deliver blood to your brain and head.

Concept note-4: -The carotid arteries are vital for providing oxygenated blood to the brain. Just like all arteries, they are susceptible to atherosclerosis, which can lead to stenosis and distal embolism of plaque.

Concept note-5: -The head and neck region obtain the majority of its blood supply via the carotid and also vertebral arteries.
