What muscle is responsible for drawing the corners of the mouth upwards or downwards to smile or frown?

(A) ** Zygomaticus

(B) Buccinator

(C) Masseter

(D) Sternocleidomastoid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The happy muscle, makes the corners of our mouth turn upwards into a smile.

Concept note-2: -Technically, the zygomaticus muscles contract to draw the corners of the mouth upward and outward to create your smile. If the zygomaticus muscle splits at birth, it creates another pleasing physical characteristic – dimples.

Concept note-3: -The levator anguli oris (caninus) inserts at the corners of the mouth at an angle, and is associated with other muscles including the zygomaticus, triangularis, and orbicularis oris. When innervated, this muscle contracts to lift the corners of the mouth, producing part of the expression of a smile.

Concept note-4: -Levator anguli oris muscle As its name suggests, the main function of the levator anguli oris is to elevate the angles of the lips, thereby contributing to producing a smile, together with the risorius, zygomaticus major and minor.

Concept note-5: -Of all the muscles in the face, the zygomaticus major is perhaps the most noticeable. Sitting between the corners of our lips and the upper part of our cheeks, it controls the way in which we smile. The muscle sits atop the zygomatic bone, otherwise known as the cheekbone.
