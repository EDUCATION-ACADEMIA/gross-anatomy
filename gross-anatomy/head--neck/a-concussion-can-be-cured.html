A concussion can be cured?

(A) ** Yes, most concussions resolve on their own

(B) No, concussions are incurable

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Concussions are a mild form of traumatic brain injury (TBI), and most concussed people recover fully with no long-term complications. Concussions often heal on their own with rest. It may be necessary to take a break from sports, school, or work while recovering, or receive accommodations.

Concept note-2: -Most concussions resolve on their own without any lasting effects. However, some concussions can accompany a more serious injury that requires treatment.

Concept note-3: -Your doctor will recommend that you physically and mentally rest to recover from a concussion. Relative rest, which includes limiting activities that require thinking and mental concentration, is recommended for the first two days after a concussion.

Concept note-4: -Approximately 80 percent of concussions resolve over seven to 14 days, with an average of 10 days. People with concussions should never return to sports or other physical activity sooner than one week from sustaining the injury.

Concept note-5: -Most people with concussions will experience a complete recovery, but the length of time it takes the body and mind to heal can vary. In most cases, someone will recover within a month. In rare cases, recovery can take longer than 6 weeks, which is known as post-concussion syndrome.
