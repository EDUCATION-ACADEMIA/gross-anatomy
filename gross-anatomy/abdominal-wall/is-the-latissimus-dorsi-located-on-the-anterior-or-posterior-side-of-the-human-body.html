Is the latissimus dorsi located on the anterior or posterior side of the human body?

(A) anterior

(B) ** posterior

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The latissimus dorsi muscle is a broad, flat muscle that occupies the majority of the lower posterior thorax. The muscleâ€™s primary function is of the upper extremity but is also considered to be a respiratory accessory muscle.

Concept note-2: -The latissimus dorsi is the larger, flat, dorso-lateral muscle on the trunk, posterior to the arm, and partly covered by the trapezius on its median dorsal region.

Concept note-3: -The latissimus dorsi muscle spans over the lumbar and lower thoracic regions of the back. Depending on the origin, one can distinguish four parts of this muscle: Vertebral part: originating from the spinous processes of the 7th to 12th thoracic vertebrae and the thoracolumbar fascia.

Concept note-4: -The latissimus dorsi is considered a muscle contributing to both thoracic and brachial (i.e. arm) motion. The muscle has attachments to the lower six vertebral spinous processes anterior to the attachment of the trapezius on the spinous processes.

Concept note-5: -The anterior border of the latissimus dorsi muscle is located along a line between the posterior axillary fold and the iliac spine. In thin patients, the border of the muscle can be palpated in the direction from the humerus in an oblique line to the ribs.
