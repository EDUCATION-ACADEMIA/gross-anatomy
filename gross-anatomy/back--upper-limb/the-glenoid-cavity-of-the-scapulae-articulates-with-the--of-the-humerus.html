The glenoid cavity of the scapulae articulates with the ____ of the humerus

(A) sugerical neck

(B) ** head

(C) styloid process

(D) body

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -…presents a shallow cavity, the glenoid cavity, which articulates with the head of the bone of the upper arm, the humerus, to form the shoulder joint. Overhanging the glenoid cavity is a beaklike projection, the coracoid process, which completes the shoulder socket.

Concept note-2: -The glenoid cavity of the scapula articulates with the head of the humerus, the upper-arm bone at the shoulder joint, enabling the arm to move relative to the torso.

Concept note-3: -The head of the humerus articulates with the glenoid cavity to form the shoulder pint. The humerus bone is located in the forelimb. The head of the humerus bone attaches or fits in the scapula at the glenoid cavity, forming 360 degrees rotating joint(ball and socket joint).

Concept note-4: -Head of humerus bone articulates with the glenoid cavity of pectoral girdle. This articulation results in the formation of ball and socket joints, e.g., ball and socket joints present in shoulder while other statement are incorrect.

Concept note-5: -The humerus articulates with the scapula proximally at the glenohumeral joint so it participates in the movements of the shoulder.
