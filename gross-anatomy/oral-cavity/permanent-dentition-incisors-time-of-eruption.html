permanent dentition incisors time of eruption

(A) ** 3.5-4 months

(B) 5-6 months

(C) 4-5 months

(D) 5-7 months

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Generally, the order of and rough timeline for each type of permanent tooth is: First molars – between 6 and 7 years. Central incisors – between 6 and 8 years. Lateral incisors – between 7 and 8 years.

Concept note-2: -Once the baby tooth has fallen out it can take as long as six months for the permanent adult tooth to appear in its place. Sometimes the gap can remain unfilled for a lot longer, causing concern in parents about the development of their child’s teeth.

Concept note-3: -The sequences of eruption of permanent teeth were 6-1-2-4-3-5-7 in mandible, and 6-1-2-4-5-3-7 in maxilla in boys whereas 1-6-2-3-4-5-7 in mandible, and 6-1-2-5-4-3-7 in maxilla in girls.

Concept note-4: -Eruption Patterns The first teeth to erupt in the oral cavity are the deciduous mandibular central incisors at approximately 5 to 8 months of age, followed by maxillary central incisors a month or two later. In general, by the age of 19 months, the child has a total of 12 erupted deciduous teeth.
