Which of the following can be renewed as adults?

(A) ** Cementum

(B) ** Periodontal lig.

(C) ** Dentin

(D) Enamel

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Reparative or tertiary dentine forms as a result of trauma to the odontoblasts; this can be thermal, chemical, bacterial or mechanical. Tertiary dentine has few tubules and is darker in colour and very dense in structure.

Concept note-2: -Tooth enamel is incapable of self-repairing whereas dentin and cememtum can regenerate with limited capacity.

Concept note-3: -The dentine of older people is characterized by the continuous narrowing of the lumen of the dentinal tubule, increasing calcification, reduction in the amount of peritubular fluid and reduced sensitivity. In this process, dentine becomes able to assume the function of enamel as it wears.

Concept note-4: -The potential of pulp tissue to regenerate lost dentin is well known. When pulp tissue is exposed owing to the loss of the overlaying dentin, direct pulp-capping therapy can allow the pulp to form new dentin, which is termed a dentin bridge.
