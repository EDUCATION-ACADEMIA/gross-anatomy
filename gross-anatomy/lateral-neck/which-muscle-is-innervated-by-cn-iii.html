Which muscle is innervated by CN III

(A) Orbicularis oculi

(B) Orbicularis oris

(C) Buccinator

(D) ** Levator palpebrae superioris

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The oculomotor nerve controls several muscles: Levator palpebrae superioris-raises the upper eyelid. Superior rectus muscle-rotates the eyeball backward, “looking up"

Concept note-2: -Background: The levator palpebrae superioris muscle (LPS) acts as the upper eyelid’s major elevator and retractor and is innervated by the oculomotor nerve.

Concept note-3: -The levator palpebrae superioris muscle origin is the periosteum of the lesser wing of the sphenoid bone, superior to the optic foramen. The muscle travels anteriorly along the superior aspect of the orbit superior to the superior rectus muscle.

Concept note-4: -Eyelid muscle innervation is achieved by cranial nerve VII (the facial nerve), cranial nerve III (the oculomotor nerve), and sympathetic nerve fibers. The facial nerve (CNVII) innervates the orbicularis oculi, frontalis, procerus, and corrugator supercilii muscles, and supports eyelid protraction.

Concept note-5: -The levator palpebrae superioris originates on the lesser wing of the sphenoid bone, just above the optic foramen. It broadens and becomes the levator aponeurosis. This portion inserts on the skin of the upper eyelid, as well as the superior tarsal plate. It is a skeletal muscle.
