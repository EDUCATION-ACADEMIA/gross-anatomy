EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The C3 pain dermatome is a craniofacial area including the scalp around the ear, the pinna, the lateral cheek over the angle of the jaw, the submental region and the lateral and anterior aspects of the upper neck.

Concept note-2: -The C3 spinal nerves are responsible for sensation in the neck area as well as neck side flexion, which is the ability to tilt your neck to each side. As a result, individuals with C3 spinal cord injuries should be able to feel and move their head and neck normally.

Concept note-3: -The transverse cervical nerve, of C2 and C3, projects posteriorly and wraps around anteriorly to supply the skin of the anterior cervical region. The supraclavicular nerve, of C3 and C4, descends from the posterior sternocleidomastoid and provides sensory information from the skin over the clavicle and shoulder.

Concept note-4: -For people who do, this dermatome is at the center of the back of their head. C2 to C3: Upper neck, a small area of your jaw underneath each ear and back of your head. C3 to C4: Lower neck, upper chest and upper back. C4 to C5: Shoulders and upper arms.
