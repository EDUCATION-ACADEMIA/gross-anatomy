THE PARIETAL LOBES ARE TO THE ____ AS THE OCCIPITAL LOBES ARE TO:

(A) HEARING AND SPEAKING

(B) ** SENSING TOUCH AND SEEING

(C) TASTING AND SMELLING

(D) SPEAKING AND SEEING

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Both parietal and occipital areas seem to contribute to the perception of straight ahead body orientation and seem to have counteracting effects when lesioned in the same hemisphere.

Concept note-2: -The parietal lobe is one of the four major lobes of the cortex. It is primarily responsible for sensations of touch, such as temperature and pain, but it also plays a role in numerous other functions.

Concept note-3: -Your parietal lobe is a processing center for sensations you can feel with your sense of touch. These include temperature (hot and cold), pressure, vibration and pain.
