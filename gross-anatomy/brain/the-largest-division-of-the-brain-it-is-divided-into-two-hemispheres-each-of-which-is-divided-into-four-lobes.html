BRAIN

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

The largest division of the brain. It is divided into two hemispheres, each of which is divided into four lobes

(A) Cerebellum

(B) ** Cerebrum

(C) Sulcus

(D) Gyrus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your cerebrum is the largest area of your brain. Your cerebrum divides your brain into two halves called hemispheres. The hemispheres are attached by a bundle of nerve fibers called the corpus callosum. The corpus callosum allows your two hemispheres to communicate with each other.

Concept note-2: -There are four lobes in the cortex, the frontal lobe, parietal lobe, temporal lobe, occipital lobe.

Concept note-3: -The cerebrum is divided into four lobes: frontal, parietal, occipital and temporal.

Concept note-4: -The cerebral cortex is divided lengthways into two cerebral hemispheres connected by the corpus callosum. Traditionally, each of the hemispheres has been divided into four lobes: frontal, parietal, temporal and occipital.

Concept note-5: -The largest part of the brain. It is divided into two hemispheres, or halves, called the cerebral hemispheres. Areas within the cerebrum control muscle functions and also control speech, thought, emotions, reading, writing, and learning.
