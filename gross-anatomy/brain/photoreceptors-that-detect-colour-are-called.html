Photoreceptors that detect colour are called..

(A) ** Cones

(B) Rods

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cone cells, or cones, are photoreceptor cells in the retinas of vertebrate eyes including the human eye. They respond differently to light of different wavelengths, and the combination of their responses is responsible for color vision.

Concept note-2: -There are 2 types of photoreceptors: rods, which detect dim light and are used for night vision, and cones, which detect different colors and require brightly lit environments.

Concept note-3: -Cones are responsible for color vision. Every green mark on this image is a specific ion channel necessary for the function of these cones. The cones marked in magenta are responsible for detecting colors on the spectrum from red to green. The cones marked in blue are responsible for detecting the color blue.

Concept note-4: -Rods are responsible for vision at low light levels (scotopic vision). They do not mediate color vision, and have a low spatial acuity. Cones are active at higher light levels (photopic vision), are capable of color vision and are responsible for high spatial acuity.

Concept note-5: -Cone cells help detect colors. Most people have three kinds of cone cells. People without all three see fewer colors, sometimes called color blindness. Some cones respond more strongly to blue light.
