EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Amongst these, magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is probably the most preferred choice as it enables mapping of neural events with excellent specificity, spatiotemporal resolution and unlimited tissue penetration. Its functional variant, fMRI, is currently the mainstay of neuroimaging.

Concept note-2: -Stereotaxis is the process by which neurosurgeons use MRI or CT imaging studies, targeted algorithms and a computer workstation to precisely locate and target a tumor or other lesion inside the brain. Previously, this was done by placing a metal frame on a patients head.

Concept note-3: -Amygdala is the integrative center for emotions, emotional behavior, and motivation. If the brain is turned upside down the end of the structure continuous with the hippocampus is called the uncus.

Concept note-4: -Action potentials either happen or they don’t; there is no such thing as a “partial” firing of a neuron. This principle is known as the all-or-none law. This means that neurons always fire at their full strength.
