BRAIN

[SOURCES]
The ability to purposefully focus our attention and filter out outside distractions is called

(A) ** The Cocktail Party Effect

(B) The Invisible Gorilla Illusion

(C) The Illusion of Attention

(D) The Illusion of Memory

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The cocktail party effect is the phenomenon of the brain’s ability to focus one’s auditory attention on a particular stimulus while filtering out a range of other stimuli, such as when a partygoer can focus on a single conversation in a noisy room.

Concept note-2: -The cocktail-party effect refers to the ability to focus one’s attention a particular stimulus while filtering out a range of other stimuli (i.e., noise).

Concept note-3: -The cocktail party effect refers to the ability of people to focus on a single talker or conversation in a noisy environment. For example, if you are talking to a friend at a noisy party, you are able to listen and understand what they are talking about – and ignore what other people nearby are saying.

Concept note-4: -A cocktail party is a party at which cocktails are served. It is sometimes called a cocktail reception.

Concept note-5: -Other researchers have demonstrated the “ cocktail party effect ” (Cherry, 1953) under experimental conditions and have discovered occasions when information heard in the unattended ear “broke through” to interfere with information participants are paying attention to in the other ear.
