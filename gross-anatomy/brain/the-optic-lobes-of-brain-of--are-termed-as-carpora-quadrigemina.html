The optic lobes of brain of ____ are termed as carpora quadrigemina.

(A) ** mammals

(B) reptiles

(C) ambhibians

(D) fishes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The corpora quadrigemina in mammals are four solid lobes on the dorsal side of the cerebral aqueduct, where the superior posterior pair are called as the superior colliculi and the inferior posterior pair is called the inferior colliculi. The four solid lobes help to decussate several fibres of the optic nerve.

Concept note-2: -In mammals, they are 4 in number (corpora quadrigemina) called inferior corpora or inferior colliculi. They are solid without an optocoel. They are concerned with both vision and hearing. They control reflexes from the cavity of the middle ear.

Concept note-3: -The mid-brain or mesencephalon consists of optic lobes and crura ceribri. In frog, there are two optic lobes (corpora bigemina) which are hollow but in man, there are four solid optic lobes lying behind the dicencephalon. They collectively constitute the corpora quadrigemina.

Concept note-4: -The midbrain has two parts, the tectum and the tegmentum, which are separated by the aqueduct of Sylvius, or cerebral aqueduct. The tectum, located dorsal (toward the back) to the aqueduct of Sylvius, is comprised of four rounded protrusions termed the corpora quadrigemina.

Concept note-5: -The dorsal region of this portion of the brain has four lobes that are known as corpora quadrigemina. It is the reflex center of vision and hearing. They are 4 colliculi (2 inferior and 2 superior) that are present on a quadrigeminal plate.
