BRAIN

Front of the brain; especially involved in movement and thinking

(A) Occipital Lobe

(B) ** Frontal Lobe

(C) Parietal Lobe

(D) Temporal Lobe

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The frontal lobe is the front-most part of your brain. It’s responsible for control over many abilities, including the way you think, how you move and how you remember things. It’s also a key part in your social skills, and helps you understand and control how you talk, behave and interact with others.

Concept note-2: -Speech and language production: Broca’s area, a region in the frontal lobe, helps put thoughts into words.

Concept note-3: -The frontal lobes are important for voluntary movement, expressive language and for managing higher level executive functions.

Concept note-4: -The frontal lobe is the most anterior (front) part of the brain. It extends from the area behind the forehead back to the precentral gyrus. As a whole, the frontal lobe is responsible for higher cognitive functions such as memory, emotions, impulse control, problem solving, social interaction, and motor function.

Concept note-5: -The cerebrum (front of brain) comprises gray matter (the cerebral cortex) and white matter at its center. The largest part of the brain, the cerebrum initiates and coordinates movement and regulates temperature.
